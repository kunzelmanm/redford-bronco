Bought the Bronco on a Thursday. Had it on the rocks by Saturday.

Sand Hollow State Park sits about 20 minutes from Hurricane — close enough that you can decide at breakfast and be aired down by lunch. The park's known for two things: red slickrock that'll test your angles, and sand so soft it swallows stock SUVs whole.

I figured the Badlands Sasquatch package meant I was ready. Factory 35" Goodyear MTRs, front and rear electronic lockers, and the Hoss 3.0 suspension. The salesman said it was "trail-ready out of the box."

## The Shakedown Run

Picked a moderate route — or what the guy at the gas station called "moderate for a Sasquatch." Started easy: packed dirt, mild inclines, nothing the Subaru couldn't have handled. Then the trail opened onto slickrock.

This is where the Bronco woke up.

> "Red slickrock doesn't care about your build sheet. It cares about contact patches and confidence."

Locked the front diff. Crawl control on. Picked a line over a ledge that looked manageable until I was committed. The rear end hopped. Front tires clawed. Factory skid plate scraped hard enough to make me wince. And then I was over.

### What Actually Worked

- **35" tires** — Had way more clearance than expected. Didn't scrape the bumper once.
- **Rear locker** — Used it constantly. Front locker maybe twice.
- **Trail Turn Assist** — Worked better than I thought it would. Made U-turns on narrow shelves way less stressful.
- **Stock suspension travel** — Articulated better than my old lifted Jeep. Go figure.

### What Didn't

- **Me** — Picked terrible lines three times. Learned the hard way that slickrock punishes indecision.
- **Stock approach angle** — It's fine, but that front bumper is *right there*.

## The Sand Pit

After two hours of slickrock, hit the sand. Big, open bowl of it. Aired down to 18 PSI and just pinned it.

The Bronco floated. EcoBoost didn't hunt for gears. Just pulled. Made a few passes, threw some rooster tails, pretended I knew what I was doing. No issues.

Then I tried to air back up and realized I forgot my compressor.

Limped out at 18 PSI, hit the gas station, and spent 20 minutes with a coin-op air hose like an idiot. Lesson learned: gear checklist exists for a reason.

## The Verdict

Sand Hollow is a perfect first run. Close, forgiving if you stick to the marked routes, and technical enough to actually learn something. The Bronco handled it all stock.

Would I go back? Already planning it.

**Trail Stats:**
- **Distance:** ~12 miles
- **Duration:** 3.5 hours (including lunch)
- **Difficulty:** Moderate
- **Damage:** One scraped skid plate, zero regrets
