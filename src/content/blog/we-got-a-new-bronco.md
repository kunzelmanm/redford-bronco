After months of research and waiting, we finally pulled the trigger on a 2025 Ford Bronco Badlands Sasquatch. Ruby Red Metallic with the color-matched hardtop — the spec I'd been dreaming about since the Bronco came back.

## The Build

**2025 Ford Bronco Badlands Sasquatch**

The Sasquatch package was non-negotiable. 35-inch Goodyear Territory MT tires on beadlock-capable wheels, 4.70 gears, electronic locking front and rear differentials, and Bilstein position-sensitive monotube shocks. This thing came ready to crawl right off the lot.

But we didn't stop there.

### HOSS 3.0 Suspension

The factory Sasquatch suspension is solid, but the HOSS 3.0 system takes it to another level. Tuned specifically for the Bronco, it delivers better articulation and control on technical terrain while still being civilized on-road. The difference is immediately noticeable — more travel, better damping, and the confidence to tackle bigger obstacles.

### Raptor Running Boards

Practical meets aggressive. The Raptor-style running boards give you the step you need for entry and exit (especially important with 35s) while maintaining decent ground clearance. They're built tough enough to take a beating on the rocks without folding up like cheap rocker guards.

### Storage Solutions

Southern Utah trails mean you're carrying extra gear — recovery equipment, water, tools, spare parts. We added Ford's door storage bags and a roof storage bag to keep everything organized and accessible. The door bags are perfect for trail essentials you need quick access to, and the roof bag keeps bulkier items secure without eating up cargo space inside.

### The Color

Ruby Red Metallic with the color-matched hardtop. There's something about a red Bronco against red rock that just works. The matched hardtop was a must — keeps the lines clean and the whole rig feels more cohesive than mixing body and top colors.

## What's Next

The Bronco's already trail-ready, but there's always room for improvement. Considering a winch setup for self-recovery, upgrading the lighting for night runs, and possibly a roof rack system for longer expeditions. But for now, it's time to break it in properly.

First stop: Sand Hollow State Park.
